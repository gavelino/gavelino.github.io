## Course Overview

This graduate-level course covers empirical research methods in the context of software engineering. By the end of this course, students will be able to:

- Understand the different types of empirical studies used in software engineering research
- Design and execute a small-scale empirical study on a software engineering topic
- Mine and analyze data from software repositories (e.g., GitHub, issue trackers)
- Critically read and evaluate empirical software engineering papers
- Present and communicate research findings clearly

## Prerequisites

- Graduate standing in Computer Science or Software Engineering
- Basic knowledge of statistics and probability
- Programming experience in Python or R (for data analysis)

## Textbooks

- Primary: "Guide to Advanced Empirical Software Engineering" by Forrest Shull, Janice Singer & Dag I.K. Sjøberg
- Reference: "Case Study Research in Software Engineering" by Per Runeson & Martin Höst
